Domain: Eukarytoa

Class: Mammalia
Order: Carnivora
Family: Felidae
Genus: Panthera
Species: P. tigris
Size: 5-9ft (1.4-2.8m)
Weight: 275-550lb(125-240kg)
Conservation Status: Endangered
Diet: Deer, wild pigs, ground birds
Habitat: Temperate/tropical forests, wetlands, and grasslands
Range:
  • Like human fingerprints, each tiger's stripe pattern is unique. This is especially helpful for consveration efforts as it allows for researchers to track individual tigers.
  • Six subspecies are alive today, ranging from the temperate forests of Siberia to the tropical forests of India and southeast Asia. The Bengal tiger is the most common and well known of these subspecies. The South China subspecies, on the other hand, is believed to be extinct in the wild, as there have been no confirmed sightings since 1970.
  • Tigers are mostly a nocturnal species, but will hunt during the day in areas undisturbed by human activities.
