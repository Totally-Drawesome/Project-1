  • Galapagos torotoises are one of only two living species of giant torotoise; the other is the Aldabra tortoise of the Seychelles islands in the Indian Ocean.
  • The size and shape of Galapagos tortoises vary between islands. These distinctions were observed by Charles Darwin during the second voyage of the Beagle in 1835, helping contribute to his theory of evolution.
  • Galapagos tortoises numbers decreased drastically due to overexploitation for meat and oil, as well as the introduction of non-native species like goats, rats, and pigs to the islands. Only 12 subspecies survive today, with three populations having been declared extinct.
