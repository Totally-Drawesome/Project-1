  • Though zebras are often referred to as "wild horses," they are actually more closely related to donkeys.
  • While the plains zebra is not considered endangered, the quagga subspecies of South Africa was driven to extinction in the late 19th century. The seven remaining subspecies continue to be threatened hunting and habitat loss.
  • A zebra's stripes are often described as a form of camouflage, some scientists believe that they serve as a form of insect repellent; the black-and-white stripes disrupt flies' flying patterns, ultimately rendering them unable to land.
