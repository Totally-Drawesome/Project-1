Eudyptes robustus

Size: 19.5-27.5in (50-70cm)
Weight: 5.5-8.8lb (2.5-5kg)
Conservation Status: Vulnerable
Diet: Krill, squid, and small fish
Habitat: Coasts
Range:
  • Colonies of Snares penguins can range from 10 to 1,200 nests.
  • The Snares penguin is visually similar to most other species of the crested penguin genus. It is most often compared to the Fiordland penguin of New Zealand's South Island, but can be distinguished from the latter species by a patch of pink skin at the base of its bill.
  • While the Snares penguin population is not currently endangered, the species could be easily wiped out should a new threat arrive to the islands, such as an introduced predator or rising sea levels. The government of New Zealand has taken preventative measures to protect these penguins and their breeding grounds from any possible threats.
