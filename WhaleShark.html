WHALE SHARK

Rhincodon typus



Domain: Eukarytoa
Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Chondrichthyes
Order: Orectolobiformes
Family: Rhincodontidae
Genus: Rhincodon
Species: R. typus
Size: 40ft (12m)
Weight: 13.2 tons (12 metric tons)
Conservation Status: Endangered
Diet: Phytoplankton and zooplankton
Habitat: Temperate/tropical oceans
Range:
  • The whale shark holds many records in the animal kingdom for its size alone. It is the largest living fish, as well as the third largest living sea creature after the fin whale and the blue whale.
  • Despite the reputation of many shark species as ferocious man-eaters, the whale shark is a gentle giant. They only eat plankton and small fish, and are docile towards humans, even allowing swimmers to catch a ride on their back. Many scientists however advise against this behavior, as whale sharks are still wild animals that deserve their personal space.
  • In the wild, it is believed that whale sharks have an average lifespan of 100-150 years. Unfortunately very few whale sharks actually reach adulthood, due to the fact that it takes them 30 years to sexually mature, as well as the looming threats of overfishing, poaching, and pollution.
