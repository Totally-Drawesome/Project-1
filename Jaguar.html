JAGUAR

Panthera onca



Domain: Eukarytoa
Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Mammalia
Order: Carnivora
Family: Felidae
Genus: Panthera
Species: P. onca
Size: 4-6ft (1.1-1.7m)
Weight: 70-270lb (32-122kg)
Conservation Status: Near Threatened
Diet: Mammals, reptiles, and birds
Habitat: Temperate/tropical forests, wetlands, and grasslands
Range:
  • Jaguars have a bite force of 1,500 pounds per square inch, the most powerful bite of any wild cat, which is capable of piercing bones and even turtle shells.
  • The melanistic gene that causes the "black panther" mutation is a dominant gene for jaguars, while for leopards the gene is recessive.
  • Jaguars previously ranged from the forests of the southwestern United States down to the pampas of Argentina, but due to significant habitat loss, as well as persecution from farmers, it currently resides in only 45% of its original range. It has been almost completely extirpated from North America, though jaguars are still occassionally spotted in Arizona.
