Size: 43-55in (109-140cm)

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
Diet: Carrion
Habitat: Deserts, forests and shrublands
Range:
  • The California condor has the longest wingspan of any North American bird, stretching three meters from tip to tip.
  • During the ice age, the California condor was widespread across North America, where it scavenged on the carcasses of mammoths and other prehistoric megafauna.
  • By the late 20th century, condor numbers had plummeted as a result of pesticides, poaching, and lead poisoning. By 1987, only only 27 individuals remained in the wild. Thankfully, through conservation programs lead by the San Diego Wild Animal Park and the Los Angeles Zoo, the surviving condors were bred together and in 1991, were released back into the wild. Though the California condor is still a threatened species, it managed to make a successful recovery from the brink of extinction.
