Habitat: Temperate/tropical oceans
Range:
  • Sea turtle populations across the globe are genetically distinct, each one having its own unique set of nesting and feeding grounds within their natural range.
  • Much of what we know about sea turtle ecology today can be attributed to studies of green sea turtles, due to them being one of the first sea turtle species studied.
  • Though sea turtles are nationally protected by law, many turtles continue to be threatened by poaching, pollution, and entanglement in fishing nets. Additionally, many beaches which have served as important nesting grounds have been lost to real estate development.
