Ara chloropterus

Size: 35-37in (90-95cm)
Weight: 2.3-3.8lbs (1.1-1.7kg)
Conservation Status: Stable
Diet: Fruits and nuts
Habitat: Tropical forests
Range:
  • The green-winged macaw is the second largest parrot species, surpassed in size only by the hyacinth macaw.
  • It is often confused for the scarlet macaw, though the two species can be readily distinguished from one another. Unlike the scarlet macaw, which has yellow feathers on its upper wing and a mostly bare white skin patch, the green-winged macaw has green feathers on its upper wing and distinct red lines around its eye.
  • While the green-winged macaw remains common throughout tropical South America, its wild numbers have been declining recently due to habitat loss and illegal pet trade.
