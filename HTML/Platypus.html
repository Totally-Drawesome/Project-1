Domain: Eukarytoa

Genus: Ornithorhynchus

Size: 16-22in (41-56cm)
Weight: 2-5lb (1-2.3kg)
Conservation Status: Near Threatened
Diet: Insect larvae and crayfish
Habitat: Wetlands
Range:
  • Platypuses, like their cousins, the echidnas, are the only extant egg-laying mammals on Earth. Female platypuses produce milk, but because they lack nipples, the milk is secreted through pores in the skin, which is lapped up by their young.
  • Platypuses are one of the few species of of venomous mammal, with males possessing a sharp spur on each hind foot. The venom in these spurs is powerful enough to incapacitate a human or kill a small animal.
  • When a dead specimen was first delivered to Europe in 1798, British scientists initally dismissed it as a hoax, believing that a taxidermist had sewn the bill of a duck onto the body of a beaver-like animal. They even looked for stitches in its fur to see if the specimen had been sewn together.
