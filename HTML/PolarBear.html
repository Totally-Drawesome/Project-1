POLAR BEAR

Ursus maritimus



Domain: Eukarytoa

Genus: Ursus
Species: U. maritimus
Size: 6-9ft (1.8-2.8m)
Weight: 880-1,500lb (400-680kg)
Conservation Status: Vulnerable
Diet: Seals, fish, birds, and vegetation
Habitat: Arctic coasts and tundra
Range:
  • Polar bears are the largest extant land carnivore on Earth. They are closely related to the brown bear, with the two species having been known to hybridize since the Pleistocene.
  • The fur of the polar bear is not truly white; instead, it is clear and hallow, allowing light to reflect off and provide the bear with the kind of camouflage it needs in an Arctic environment.
  • Polar bears are classified as marine mammals, due to their habit of hunting on the annual sea ice for prey such as ringed seals and beluga whales. As a result of climate change, however, much of the annual sea ice that the bears rely upon has been melting, resulting in these bears moving further inland where they come into conflict with humans.
