AFRICAN FOREST ELEPHANT

Loxodonta cyclotis



Domain: Eukarytoa
Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Mammalia
Order: Proboscidea
Family: Elephantidae
Genus: Loxodonta
Species: L. cyclotis
Size: 10-13ft (3-4m)
Weight: 8,800-15,400lb (4,000-7,000kg)
Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
Diet: Fruits, leaves, and tree bark
Habitat: Tropical Forest
Range:
  • African forest elephants are the smallest of the three elephant species.
  • Unlike their cousins of the savannah, forest elephants have straight, downward-facing tusks, as well as more rounded ears.
  • While the forest elephant was first scientifically described back in 1900, it was believed to be a subspecies of the African bush elephant until 2021, when it was finally classified as its own species. Unfortunetly, the species' numbers have drastically reduced due to years of poaching, habitat loss, and human conflict, ultimately resulting in the forest elephant being classified on the IUCN Red List as an endangered species.
