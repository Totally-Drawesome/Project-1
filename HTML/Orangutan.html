BORNEAN ORANGUTAN

Pongo pygmaeus



Domain: Eukarytoa
Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Mammalia
Order: Primates
Family: Hominidae
Genus: Pongo
Species: P. pygmaeus
Size: 4-5ft (1.1-1.4m)
Weight: 88-176lb (40-80kg)
Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
Diet: Plants, eggs, and insects
Habitat: Tropical forests
Range:
  • Bornean orangutans are the largest arboreal animals on Earth; in spite of this, they spend more time on the ground than the Sumatran orangutan, likely due to the lack of tigers in Borneo.
  • Male orangutans sport large cheek pads known as flanges, which are used to both attract females and intimidate other males.
  • The orangutan's red fur serves as a source of camouflage; their natural predator, the clouded leopard, has dichromatic vision, meaning it is unable to see the color red.
