Genus: Phascolarctos

Size: 26-32in (65-82)cm

Conservation Status: Vulnerable
Diet: Eucalyptus leaves
Habitat: Temperate forests
Range:
  • Koalas obtain moisture through the consumption of eucalyptus leaves. They feed exclusively on these leaves, having evolved to eat a plant that would poison most other animals.
  • Despite being an arboreal marsupial, koalas possess downward facing pouches. Scientists believed that koalas evolved to have downward pouches from when they used to burrow underground, though koalas stop burrowing centuries ago.
  • Along with kangaroos and emus, koalas are one of Australia's most iconic species. In the wild, however, they are threatened by habitat loss, as well as droughts and bushfires caused by climate change.
