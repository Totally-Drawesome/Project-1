BURROWING OWL

Athene cunicularia



Domain: Eukarytoa

Phylum: Chordata
Class: Aves
Order: Strigiformes
Family: Strigidae
Genus: Athene
Species: A. cunicularia
Size: 8-10in (20-25cm)
Weight: 5-9oz (141-255g)
Conservation Status: Stable
Diet: Small invertebrates and rodents
Habitat: Grasslands and deserts
Range:
  • Burrowing owls roost and nest in burrows that have been excavated by other mammals, such as prarie dogs and ground squirrels.
  • To ward off predators, burrowing owls produce hissing and rattling sounds akin to those of a rattlesnake; this strategy is known as Batesian mimicry.
  • Although the burrowing owl remains common in many Neotropical countries, it is considered a threatened species in North America. A significant reason for this is habitat loss, as well as the eradication of many burrowing animals upon which the burrowing owl depends. Without a burrow to call home, these owls become more vulnerable to predation from badgers, coyotes, and rattlesnakes.
